Our flagship programme

As organisations prepare for the next generation of early-career professionals, many are recognising the importance of establishing strong foundations for responsible AI use from the outset.

Flagship Full-Day Course

Responsible AI Use for Early-Career Professionals

A full-day course that gives early-career employees the judgement and practical skills to use AI tools well, spot what they get wrong, and be open about how AI supports their work.

Full day In-person or online 6 learning objectives Interactive

Learning Objectives Covered

This course covers six objectives across Capability and Behaviour:

Understand how generative AI works, where it adds value, and where it introduces risk. Recognise the professional consequences of relying on AI outputs without appropriate scrutiny.

Develop practical techniques to identify errors, challenge assumptions, and validate AI-generated content, supporting ownership of output quality.

Build awareness of governance, confidentiality, and data-security considerations when using AI tools, including appropriate handling of sensitive or proprietary information.

Understand the differences between publicly available and organisation-specific AI tools, enabling informed decisions about when and how AI should be used.

Develop practical skills in structuring prompts, refining outputs, and interacting productively with AI tools to support high-quality work.

Encourage open and responsible communication around AI use, supporting trust, consistency, and shared understanding across teams.

01

Foundations of Responsible AI Use

Morning session. Focus: understanding risk, strengthening judgement, and applying due diligence when working with AI-generated information.

  • Introduction to responsible AI use in professional environments
  • Participant insight survey on current AI behaviours
  • Overview of AI adoption trends and emerging workplace expectations
  • Live demonstrations illustrating AI inaccuracies and limitations
  • Interactive quiz exploring reliability of information
  • Explanation of how generative AI produces outputs
  • Practical approaches to validating AI-generated content
  • Group challenge applying structured due diligence techniques
  • Discussion of organisational and professional risks
02

Applying AI Effectively and Transparently at Work

Afternoon session. Focus: developing practical capability in selecting appropriate tools, structuring prompts, and integrating AI responsibly into workflows.

  • Overview of available AI tools and typical use cases
  • Guidance on selecting the right AI tool for different types of work
  • Practical techniques for effective prompt structuring
  • Live demonstration of prompt refinement and output improvement
  • Interactive workshop applying prompt techniques
  • Discussion of transparent AI use as professional best practice
  • Scenario-based exercises exploring responsible AI use
  • Practical integration exercise applying learning to realistic examples

Delivery Approach

  • Interactive quizzes and polling
  • Practical individual and group exercises
  • Facilitated discussion and reflection
  • Immediately applicable to real workplace situations
