Create a 10-slide pitch deck for AIM (AI Management Coaching).

# Who this is for
Graduate scheme owners and early-careers programme leads at mid-to-large organisations (consulting, law, finance, professional services). People who run structured 1-3 year training programmes for new graduates and are responsible for the quality, development, and risk profile of those cohorts.

# What AIM does
AIM is a specialist training provider that coaches early-career professionals AND their line managers to use AI responsibly and with judgement. Founded by a partnership of an engineer (who manages Gen Z professionals in a global consultancy) and a sociologist (who designs corporate training and studies how powerful technologies reshape professional behaviour). AIM is not an AI tools vendor — it is a structured coaching programme that addresses the skills gap between AI adoption and safe, effective professional use.

# The goal of this deck
Convince a graduate scheme owner in a single reading that:
(1) their graduates are using AI far more than the rest of their workforce,
(2) those graduates are worse than any other cohort at catching when AI gets things wrong,
(3) this creates specific business risks (commercial, reputational, legal) that land on them,
(4) AIM is the bespoke solution, and
(5) they should book a pilot for their 2026 graduate cohort.

# Central thesis
Every slide must support this single sentence: "Your graduates are using AI four times more than the rest of your workforce and they are the worst at catching when it gets things wrong. AIM trains them, and their managers, to close that gap."

# Tone of voice
Confident, concise, warm, editorial. No jargon. No exclamation marks. No emojis. Never use the phrase "leverage". Never use em dashes (use commas or periods instead). Use British English spelling (organisation, behaviour, practise when verb). Read as calm expertise, not hype.

# Visual style
Editorial and minimal, like The New Yorker or a Turnberry clubhouse brochure rather than a SaaS product page. Premium, understated, generous whitespace. Muted palette. No bright colours, no gradient backgrounds, no stock photos of grinning office workers, no icon soup.

# Brand colours
Primary: Deep navy #191959 (used for headings, stat figures, buttons)
Secondary: Cream #f9f9f7 (used for warm "opportunity" section backgrounds)
Accent: Light blue-subtle #f0f0f8 (used for information section backgrounds)
Risk red: #c0392b text on #fef5f5 background (used SPARINGLY for warning stats only)
Text: #4a4a6a for body, #7a7a96 for secondary/meta
White #ffffff for neutral section backgrounds

# Typography
Headings: Serif font, DM Serif Display or Playfair Display, weight 400, tight letter spacing (-0.02em). Generous line height 1.15-1.25.
Body and UI: Sans-serif, Inter or DM Sans, weight 300 for body paragraphs, weight 600 for small eyebrow labels.
Small eyebrow labels: UPPERCASE with wide letter-spacing (0.14em-0.16em), 11-13px, used above section headings.

# Key data sources (cite on stat slides)
- PwC 2025 Global Workforce Survey
- LSE 2025 "Bridging the Generational AI Gap"
- Wiingy 2025 Gen Z AI Usage Survey
- KPMG 2025 Global AI Trust Study
- CoAI 2025 "Gen Z Hidden AI Usage" report
- EY 2024 AI Literacy Training Needs
- Deloitte 2025 Gen Zs and Millennials at Work
- Tech Monitor 2025 Enterprise Data Leakage report

# Tagline
"Aim for better." Use it on the title slide and the closing slide.

# No-go list (do not do any of these)
- Do not include pricing or cost information anywhere
- Do not mention competing training providers by name
- Do not use emojis, exclamation marks, or em dashes
- Do not use stock photography of people at laptops
- Do not say "revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "game-changing", or "leverage"
- Do not talk down to the audience or assume they don't know what AI is
- Do not make the deck feel like a product demo; it is a narrative argument
- Do not include a team photo slide with founder headshots; founders are described in text

# Slide-by-slide specification

Each slide must use an ACTION TITLE that states the conclusion, not the topic. Topic titles describe a chart, action titles describe a takeaway. Every slide needs one big idea only.

Hook line below brand, large serif, white: "The AI training your graduates need before their next mistake reaches a client."
Footer: "A pitch for graduate scheme owners" in small uppercase sans-serif, semi-transparent white.
Tagline bottom-centre: "Aim for better."

## Slide 2: Your graduates are already using AI every day. Most of your firm doesn't realise how much.
