# AIM Website Redesign — Brainstorm

## What We're Building

A complete redesign of the AIM (AI Management Coaching) website from a single-page landing site into an 8-page website that converts graduate scheme managers and L&D leaders at mid-to-large organisations into believers. The site needs to communicate what AIM does within 5-10 seconds, build credibility through data and founder story, and drive enquiries for training programmes launching September 2026.

## Why This Approach

The current single-page site is narrative-heavy and builds slowly. For a website that converts, we need to: lead with the solution (not the problem), give the buyer clear paths to the information they need, and use interactive elements to make dense content engaging. The 8-page structure separates concerns — the homepage hooks, the deeper pages close.

## Key Decisions

- **Primary audience:** Graduate scheme managers / L&D leaders at mid-to-large orgs who feel AI risk daily and have training budget
- **Master strapline:** "Aim for better." with supporting lines ("Aim for trusted outputs", "Aim for responsible AI use") used in context
- **Homepage approach:** Lean, solution-forward scroll (5 sections only). No links or heavy functionality — the burger menu handles navigation
- **Navigation:** Burger menu → Home, Our Backstory, The Evidence, Solutions & Methods, Our Syllabus, Modules & Packages, Contact Us
- **Evidence page:** Contrast pairs (adoption stat vs. risk stat) scrolling from opposite sides with animated counters. Creative and interactive
- **Solutions page:** Audience-first entry ("For your graduates" / "For your managers" / "For your culture") with click-to-expand objectives
- **Backstory page:** Two voices (engineer + sociologist) side by side, converging into "why we founded AIM"
- **Contact page:** Structured enquiry form + book a call (embedded calendar)
- **Design feel:** Clean corporate-professional (JGC-style). Existing colour palette (navy #191959, accent blue #3d5aef, cream #f9f9f7). DM Serif Display + Inter. Generous whitespace, scroll animations
- **Clients & Testimonials:** Hidden/TBC for now

## Page-by-Page Structure

### Page 1 — Homepage (lean scroll-down, no links)
- **Hero:** Solution-forward headline (starting point: "Structured AI training that turns your graduates into professionals who use AI with judgement, not just speed.") + "Aim for better." strapline — final copy to be confirmed
- **The reality:** 3-4 headline stats, one line each
- **What we do:** C/G/B as three short cards using audience-first language — static text only, no links (burger menu is sole navigation)
- **Who we are:** One sentence on the founders
- **Get started:** Email capture, Sept 2026 note

### Page 2 — Our Backstory (two voices converging)
- Engineer's perspective — managing Gen Z, accountability, seeing the risk firsthand
- Sociologist's perspective — social media parallels, learning & development, long-term impact
- Convergence — "Why we founded AIM together"
- Click-to-expand for deeper paragraphs

### Page 3 — The Evidence (high interactivity)
- Contrast pairs scrolling in from opposite sides
- Left side: adoption/usage stat / Right side: corresponding risk stat
- Animated counters, bars filling on scroll
- Sources cited beneath each pair
- Builds to punchline: "Only 1 in 3 have been trained"

### Page 4 — Solutions & Methods (audience-first, click-to-expand)
- "For your graduates" → C.1-C.5
- "For your managers" → G.1-G.7
- "For your culture" → B.1-B.3
- Each objective expands on click with full description

### Page 5 — Our Syllabus (click-to-expand)
- Modular approach explained
- Delivery formats (in-person / online / blended)
- How objectives map to course length
- Customisation options (align to org policies, tools, sector)

### Page 6 — Modules & Packages (click-to-expand)
- Flagship course front and centre
- Module 1 (Morning): Foundations of Responsible AI Use
- Module 2 (Afternoon): Applying AI Effectively and Transparently
- Delivery approach details
- Future packages signalled

### Page 7 — Clients & Testimonials (hidden for now)

### Page 8 — Contact Us
- Structured enquiry form (name, org, role, interest area dropdown)
- Book a call (embedded calendar link)
- Direct email as fallback
